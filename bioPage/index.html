Taku Nishimatsu

INTRODUCTION

Taku Nishimatsu (he/him) is a first-year student in the Multimedia Production program at Lethbridge College. He is a passionate individual who has taken several event-organizing roles since his time in high school. Taku is currently an elected member of Student Council and Vice Chair of Academic Council at Lethbridge College.

EDUCATION & EXPERIENCE

Taku began attending Lethbridge College in 2013 and obtained a diploma in General Studies in 2015. Despite the language barrier, he was elected to be part of Student Council in his second year. He was also awarded Academic Honours and was named a Featured Convocate for his academic achievement and community involvement upon completion of his program. Between 2015 and 2018, Taku volunteered and had a few positions in English Language Centre, the ESL department at Lethbridge College, where he mainly focused on supporting international students, immigrants, and other newcomers to Canada.

Taku plans to study both multimedia and architecture for the next few years, and he has exercised his artistic skills through hobbies and work. At an early age, he built houses, automobiles, spaceships out of toy blocks and designed his own holiday cards on Microsoft Paint to entertain his families and friends. For the past several years, he has designed posters, certificates, and other types of creative documents at work. He enjoys building cities and other living spaces in video games. One of his dreams is to design and build a tiny home on wheels for his partner and himself, and his ideas are influenced by the simplicity and aesthetics of the traditional Japanese tea houses.

MORE ABOUT TAKU

Taku was born in Gunma, Japan and moved to Tokyo with his family when he was 9. He has 2 siblings and is the middle child. Taku is mostly shy and anxious but can be goofy at times, and he enjoys witty jokes and dry humour. He grew up watching superheroes on TV but realized he was not very athletic. Inspired by the stories of Thomas Edison, he decided to become an inventor at an early age. Taku first came to southern Alberta as an international student in 2013 and immediately fell in love with the people, culture, and nature. He has moved back and forth between Tokyo and southern Alberta several times.

Cooking and eating food are Taku’s favourite ways of keeping his mind occupied. Although his skills are only adequate, he spends a lot of his free time in the kitchen and enjoys trying a wide variety of meals and recipes. He has recently obtained his Nerikiri Art Instructor certificate, and plans on practicing more in his downtime. Taku likes to have his favourite music and movies on while being in the kitchen such as ABBA, Queen, the ‘90s J-pop, The Shawshank Redemption, etc.
